**Comment:**
> This is the right framing - hallucination as a bias problem, not a variance problem. The scary part is not that AI makes mistakes, but that it makes *coherent* mistakes.
>
> The adversarial multi-model approach is interesting, but I would push back on one thing: three models converging on the same wrong answer is *more* dangerous, not less, because it creates an illusion of consensus. The "blind peer review" idea is good, but there is a deeper problem - all models are trained on essentially the same data distribution, so they share the same blindspots by construction.
>
> What might work better: not just multi-model, but multi-*architecture* review. A transformer, a recurrent network, and a symbolic system looking at the same problem from genuinely different inductive biases. The diversity needs to be architectural, not just instantiation-level.
